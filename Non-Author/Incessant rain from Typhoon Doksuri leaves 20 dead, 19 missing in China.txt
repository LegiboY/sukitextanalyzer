Beijing, China - December 7, 2023

Typhoon Doksuri, a powerful and relentless storm, has wreaked havoc in southern China, leaving a trail of destruction in its wake. Incessant rain and powerful winds have resulted in widespread flooding, landslides, and the loss of human lives. As the nation grapples with the aftermath, the death toll has risen to 20, with 19 individuals still missing.

The typhoon, which made landfall in Hainan Province on December 4, has since traveled inland, affecting several provinces including Guangdong, Guangxi, and Yunnan. The torrential rain that followed has caused rivers to overflow, inundating communities and displacing thousands of residents.

Rescue and relief efforts have been ongoing, with emergency responders working tirelessly to provide assistance to those affected. Chinese authorities have mobilized military personnel, firefighters, and local volunteers to aid in the rescue operations. Helicopters and boats have been deployed to reach stranded individuals in the worst-hit areas.

In Guangdong Province, one of the hardest-hit regions, floodwaters have inundated homes and forced many residents to evacuate. The relentless rain has triggered landslides, adding to the already dire situation. Thousands have been displaced from their homes, seeking shelter in makeshift evacuation centers.

Local authorities are facing immense challenges in managing the disaster. Communication infrastructure has been severely affected, making it difficult to coordinate rescue efforts. Roads have been washed away or blocked by debris, hampering access to affected areas. Power outages have added to the woes of residents and rescue teams alike.

In addition to the tragic loss of life, the typhoon has caused extensive damage to agriculture and infrastructure. Crops have been destroyed, posing a threat to food security in the region. Roads, bridges, and buildings have been damaged, further complicating recovery efforts.
